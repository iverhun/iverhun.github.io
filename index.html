Passwordless Authentication with WebAuthn
Medium - @jappware Feb 16, 2024
Oleh Tokarenko Ivan Verhun

Authentication factors fall into three categories: something you know (knowledge factor), something you have (possession factor), and something you are (inherence factor). E.g., passwords and security questions belong to the knowledge factor category.

Since each type is prone to different risks, MFA methods combine multiple factor types to overlap gaps and strengthen the overall security.

But considering all the weaknesses the knowledge factor possesses, would it be enough to use only possession and inherence factors? A new specification approved by the W3C and FIDO in 2019 — Web Authentication API (also known as WebAuthn) tries to solve precisely this problem.
